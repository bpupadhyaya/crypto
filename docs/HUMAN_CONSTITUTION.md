# The Human Constitution

### A Charter for Open Chain, Open Token (OTC), and Open Wallet

**Version:** 1.0 — Ratified by the Founding Contributors
**Date:** March 25, 2026
**Status:** Living Document — Open for Amendments by the Global Community
**License:** CC BY-SA 4.0 — Free to share, adapt, and build upon

## Preamble

We, the people of Earth, recognize that:

- Every human being is born with inherent worth and the right to a life of dignity.
- The greatest investment any civilization can make is in the raising and education of its children.
- Wars, exploitation, and suffering arise when human needs go unmet and when people are denied the tools to identify and utilize the resources around them.
- When children are raised well — with love, knowledge, and community — they grow into adults who respect themselves, respect others, and contribute to the common good.
- When all people's basic needs are met, they cannot be easily manipulated toward destruction.
- The invisible labor of parents, teachers, caregivers, and communities has never been formally recognized, valued, or rewarded by any economic system in human history.
- Technology now makes it possible to see, measure, and honor every positive contribution one human makes to another.

Therefore, we establish this Constitution as the foundational charter of **Open Chain** — a blockchain designed not for financial speculation, but for the recognition and advancement of human value.

Our ultimate purpose: **World peace through better-raised, better-educated, better-supported human beings.**

## Article I — Foundational Principles

### Section 1: The Nature of Value

1. **All human interaction is value transfer.** A parent feeding a child is value transfer. A teacher explaining a concept is value transfer. A neighbor offering help is value transfer.
2. **Value can be positive or negative.** Teaching a child to read is positive value. Neglecting a child is negative value. Both must be representable.
3. **Value flows through every level of human society** — from individual to family, community, city, country, and all of humanity.
4. **No existing economic system captures the full spectrum of human value.** Financial systems track money. Open Chain tracks everything.

### Section 2: The Right to Recognition

1. Every parent's sleepless night deserves recognition.
2. Every teacher's extra hour deserves recognition.
3. Every community volunteer's effort deserves recognition.
4. Every act of kindness, mentorship, and care deserves recognition.
5. This recognition shall be expressed in **Open Token (OTC)** — the universal unit of human contribution.

### Section 3: The Path to Peace

1. Well-raised children grow into adults who can identify resources around them and utilize those resources to serve themselves and others.
2. When people's basic needs are met, they are resilient against manipulation and exploitation.
3. When all contributions to human development are visible and valued, society self-corrects — investing more in what works, less in what harms.
4. **The chain of causation is clear:** Better upbringing → Capable individuals → Met needs → Resistant to manipulation → Harmony → Peace.

## Article II — Universal Identity

### Section 1: One Human, One Identity

1. Every human being shall be entitled to a unique **Universal Identity (Universal ID)** on Open Chain.
2. Universal ID is a cryptographic identifier — functionally equivalent to a wallet address. It reveals nothing about the person's name, location, age, gender, or any other personal attribute.
3. Universal ID is verified through **privacy-preserving biometric proof** — confirming "this is a unique human being" without storing or transmitting biometric data. All biometric processing occurs on the individual's own device.
4. No central authority shall hold a registry of Universal IDs matched to real identities. The system is designed so that such a registry **cannot exist**.

### Section 2: Selective Disclosure

1. A Universal ID holder may choose to prove specific claims without revealing their full identity:
   - "I am the parent of Universal ID X" (proves relationship)
   - "I am a certified teacher" (proves credential)
   - "I reside in Region Y" (proves locality)
2. These proofs use **zero-knowledge cryptography** — the verifier learns only that the claim is true, nothing else.
3. Selective disclosure is always voluntary. No institution, government, or individual may compel disclosure beyond what the holder consents to.

### Section 3: Identity at Birth

1. A Universal ID may be registered for a newborn by their parent(s) or legal guardian(s).
2. Upon reaching the age of agency (as defined by local governance), the individual assumes full control of their Universal ID.
3. No human being shall be denied a Universal ID for any reason. It is a birthright.

## Article III — Open Token (OTC)

### Section 1: The Nature of OTC

1. **Open Token (OTC)** is the native token of Open Chain. Ticker symbol: **OTC**.
2. OTC represents quantified human contribution — not speculative financial value.
3. OTC is **multi-dimensional**. A single number cannot capture the richness of human contribution. Therefore, OTC flows through distinct **Value Channels**:

| Channel | Symbol | Represents |
|---------|--------|------------|
| **Nurture** | nOTC | Parenting, caregiving, emotional support, child-rearing |
| **Education** | eOTC | Teaching, mentoring, knowledge transfer, skill development |
| **Health** | hOTC | Healthcare, wellness, nutrition, physical development |
| **Community** | cOTC | Neighborhood service, volunteer work, social cohesion |
| **Economic** | xOTC | Financial transactions, trade, material value exchange |
| **Governance** | gOTC | Civic participation, voting, policy contribution, leadership |

4. Each channel has independent flow rules, minting conditions, and attribution mechanisms.
5. The **Total OTC** of a Universal ID is a weighted composite of all channels. Weights are determined by global governance (Article VI).

### Section 2: Token Distribution

1. **No pre-mine.** Zero OTC exists before the chain launches.
2. **No venture capital allocation.** Zero OTC is reserved for investors.
3. **No founder allocation.** Zero OTC is reserved for creators.
4. **100% of OTC is earned** through verified contributions — from the first block, forever.
5. A farmer in India earning nOTC for raising her children has the same opportunity as any other human being on Earth. There are no privileged positions.

### Section 3: Minting Rules

1. OTC is minted when **verifiable human outcomes** occur (see Article IV: Milestone Minting).
2. OTC is **never minted from nothing**. Every token traces back to a real human contribution.
3. The total supply of OTC grows with humanity's collective contribution. There is no arbitrary cap — as humanity does more good, more OTC exists to represent it.
4. Inflation is bounded by the rate of verified human contribution, not by monetary policy.

### Section 4: The Gratitude Transaction

1. Any Universal ID holder may send OTC to any other Universal ID as an expression of gratitude.
2. A grown child sending nOTC to their parent's Universal ID is a **Gratitude Transaction** — a formal recognition of the value received during upbringing.
3. Gratitude Transactions are voluntary, never compelled, and carry special significance on-chain (marked, visible, celebrated).
4. The ability to return value to those who raised you is a fundamental feature, not an afterthought.

## Article IV — The Living Ledger

### Section 1: Architecture

1. Every Universal ID has a **Living Ledger** — a continuous, lifelong on-chain record of value received and value given.
2. The Living Ledger is organized by Value Channels (nOTC, eOTC, hOTC, cOTC, xOTC, gOTC).
3. Value flows in both directions — received and given — creating a complete picture of each person's relationship with humanity.
4. The Living Ledger begins at Universal ID registration and continues indefinitely.

### Section 2: Contribution Rings

Value attribution follows concentric rings outward from the individual:

```
         Ring 7: Humanity (global policies, international cooperation)
       Ring 6: Country (national education, healthcare, infrastructure)
     Ring 5: City / Region (local services, environment, safety)
   Ring 4: Community (neighborhood, organizations, religious groups)
  Ring 3: Teachers & Mentors (formal and informal educators)
 Ring 2: Family (parents, siblings, extended family, guardians)
Ring 1: Self (personal growth, self-education, health choices)
```

1. When a positive human outcome is verified, OTC is minted and attributed across rings.
2. **Ripple Attribution Formula:** `attribution = base_value / (ring_distance ^ 2)`
   - Parents (Ring 2) receive ~25x more attribution than national policy (Ring 6)
   - But national policy still receives credit — because good policy contributes to good outcomes
3. The formula ensures that those closest to the individual receive the most recognition, while acknowledging that human development is a collective achievement.

### Section 3: Milestone Minting

OTC is minted when verifiable human development milestones are achieved:

**Nurture Milestones (nOTC):**
- Healthy birth
- Child thriving at age 1, 2, 3, 5
- Stable family environment maintained through adolescence
- Young adult reports positive upbringing (self-attestation at age of agency)

**Education Milestones (eOTC):**
- Literacy achieved (reading at grade level)
- Numeracy achieved
- Primary education completed
- Secondary education completed
- Vocational or higher education completed
- Lifelong learning milestones (new skills, certifications)

**Health Milestones (hOTC):**
- Vaccinations completed
- Regular health checkups maintained
- Physical fitness benchmarks met
- Mental health support accessed when needed

**Community Milestones (cOTC):**
- Community service hours contributed
- Mentorship provided to others
- Conflict resolution facilitated
- Resources shared with those in need

**Governance Milestones (gOTC):**
- Participation in governance votes
- Proposal creation and community engagement
- Public service and leadership

### Section 4: Verification Oracle Network

1. Milestones are verified by a **decentralized oracle network** — not a single authority.
2. Oracles include schools, healthcare providers, community leaders, and peer attestations.
3. No single oracle can mint OTC unilaterally. Milestones require **multi-source verification** (e.g., school records + parent attestation + peer confirmation).
4. **Privacy is paramount:** Only the achievement of a milestone is recorded on-chain, never the underlying data (no medical records, no grades, no personal details).

## Article V — Correction Mechanism

### Section 1: Negative Value

1. Human society produces negative value — neglect, abuse, exploitation, deception, harm.
2. Open Chain does not ignore this reality. **Negative OTC (-OTC)** represents harm done.
3. -OTC is applied when verified negative outcomes occur, using the same ring-based attribution.
4. The purpose of -OTC is not punishment — it is **visibility**. Making harm visible creates pressure for correction.

### Section 2: Community Consensus

1. No individual can unilaterally assign -OTC to another. False accusations must be impossible.
2. Negative attribution requires **community consensus** — multiple independent verifiers must agree.
3. The accused has the right to contest, provide evidence, and have the matter resolved through governance.
4. -OTC can be reversed if the determination was wrong.

### Section 3: Autonomous Correction

1. When a community's aggregate OTC trends negative, it signals systemic problems that need attention.
2. This creates a **self-correcting feedback loop**: resources and attention flow toward communities in need.
3. The system does not dictate solutions — it surfaces problems. Humans decide how to fix them.

## Article VI — Governance

### Section 1: One Human, One Vote

1. Every Universal ID holder has exactly one vote in Open Chain governance.
2. Voting power is **not proportional to OTC holdings**. A person with 1 OTC and a person with 1,000,000 OTC each have one vote.
3. This is democracy — not plutocracy, not technocracy, not oligarchy.

### Section 2: What Can Be Governed

1. **Channel weights** — How nOTC, eOTC, etc. are weighted in Total OTC.
2. **Milestone definitions** — What constitutes a verifiable milestone (may vary by region and culture).
3. **Ripple attribution formula** — The parameters of ring-based attribution.
4. **Oracle network membership** — Who qualifies as a verifier.
5. **Protocol upgrades** — Technical changes to Open Chain.
6. **Constitutional amendments** — Changes to this document (requires supermajority).

### Section 3: Constitutional Amendments

1. This Constitution is a **living document**. It can and should evolve as humanity learns and grows.
2. Amendments require a **two-thirds supermajority** of participating Universal ID voters.
3. Amendments cannot violate the Foundational Principles (Article I) — these are immutable.
4. The amendment process must be transparent, with a minimum 90-day public deliberation period.

## Article VII — Technical Architecture

### Section 1: Open Chain

1. Open Chain is built on **Cosmos SDK** with **CometBFT** consensus.
2. The chain is **mobile-first** — designed to run on phones, not servers.
3. **Phased decentralization:**
   - Phase 1: Mobile light validators + server relay nodes
   - Phase 2: Mobile signing validators + minimal server support
   - Phase 3: Full peer-to-peer — every phone is the network
4. **IBC (Inter-Blockchain Communication)** enables interoperability with 50+ existing chains.
5. **Post-quantum cryptography** (ML-DSA + Ed25519 hybrid) protects against future quantum computing threats.

### Section 2: Open Wallet

1. Open Wallet is the **universal interface** for interacting with Open Chain and all other blockchains.
2. It supports two modes:
   - **Simple Mode:** Accessible to anyone — icon-driven, multilingual, works on 2G networks
   - **Pro Mode:** Full features for advanced users
3. Every human can manage their Universal ID, Living Ledger, OTC balances, and governance participation through Open Wallet.
4. Open Wallet is 100% open source. Anyone can inspect, verify, and build upon it.

### Section 3: Open Token (OTC)

1. OTC is the native token of Open Chain with ticker symbol **OTC**.
2. Near-zero fixed transaction fees — not percentage-based. Sending value should cost nearly nothing.
3. Multi-channel architecture (nOTC, eOTC, hOTC, cOTC, xOTC, gOTC) implemented as chain-native modules.
4. OTC is interoperable with other blockchain ecosystems via IBC bridges.

## Article VIII — Privacy and Security

### Section 1: Privacy as a Right

1. Privacy is not a feature — it is a **fundamental human right** embedded in the protocol.
2. Universal ID reveals nothing about the holder unless they choose to disclose.
3. Value flows are visible on-chain (like all blockchains), but the real-world identity behind each Universal ID is known only to the holder.
4. If a holder chooses to prove "this Universal ID is mine," they can do so — but this is always voluntary.

### Section 2: Data Sovereignty

1. All biometric data used for Universal ID verification stays on the individual's device. It is never uploaded, transmitted, or stored externally.
2. No government, corporation, or entity can compel the protocol to reveal identity mappings — because such mappings do not exist in the system.
3. Individuals own their data. Period.

### Section 3: Security

1. Post-quantum cryptography ensures that Open Chain remains secure against future threats.
2. The multi-validator consensus model prevents any single entity from controlling the chain.
3. As the network grows to billions of phone-validators, it becomes the most decentralized — and therefore most secure — blockchain in existence.

## Article IX — Inclusivity and Access

### Section 1: Universal Access

1. Open Chain is designed for **8 billion humans** — not just the technically literate.
2. Open Wallet supports multiple languages, starting with those covering 75% of the world's population.
3. The system works on basic smartphones and low-bandwidth connections.
4. No human shall be excluded from participation due to geography, language, economic status, disability, or any other factor.

### Section 2: Cultural Respect

1. Human development milestones may vary across cultures. The system respects this diversity.
2. Regional governance bodies may define milestones appropriate to their cultural context.
3. The core protocol is universal; the application is local.

### Section 3: No Discrimination

1. Universal IDs carry no information about race, gender, religion, nationality, or any protected characteristic.
2. The protocol cannot discriminate because it has no basis for discrimination.
3. OTC is earned through contribution — nothing else.

## Article X — The Vision

### Section 1: The World We're Building

We envision a world where:

- Every parent knows their sacrifice is seen and valued by all of humanity.
- Every teacher knows their impact ripples through generations, visibly and measurably.
- Every child can see, in their Living Ledger, the accumulated love and effort that shaped them — and feel inspired to pass it forward.
- Communities compete not for wealth, but for the quality of human beings they raise.
- Nations are measured not by GDP, but by the aggregate human value their citizens create.
- War becomes irrational because every person's needs are met and every person's contributions are recognized.

### Section 2: The Invitation

This Constitution is not the final word. It is the first word.

We invite every human being — every parent, every teacher, every community leader, every programmer, every dreamer — to read this document, challenge it, improve it, and build upon it.

Open Chain is not owned by anyone. It belongs to humanity.

The code is open. The governance is open. The future is open.

**Let us raise our children better. Let us value every contribution. Let us build peace.**
